Title: General Guidance for a Mild Ankle Sprain

Section 1: Early Care
For a mild ankle sprain, reduce activity and use a wrapped cold pack for fifteen to twenty minutes at a time during the first day. Keep a cloth between the cold pack and the skin.

Section 2: Movement
Gentle ankle movement can begin as pain allows. Avoid running, jumping, or forceful stretching until walking is comfortable.

Section 3: Medication
Over-the-counter pain medicine may not be appropriate for everyone. People should follow the label and ask a pharmacist or clinician about interactions, pregnancy, kidney disease, stomach ulcers, or blood-thinning medicine.

Section 4: Urgent Symptoms
Seek prompt medical care for severe swelling, obvious deformity, numbness, inability to bear weight, worsening pain, or skin that becomes cold or blue.

This educational note is not a diagnosis and does not replace medical care.
