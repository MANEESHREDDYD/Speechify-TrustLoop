Title: Community Heat Alert Program

Section 1: Purpose
The city issues a heat alert when the forecast predicts two consecutive days above thirty-seven degrees Celsius. The alert opens cooling centers and extends library hours.

Section 2: Transportation
Free shuttle routes connect senior housing sites to cooling centers between noon and eight in the evening.

Section 3: Outdoor Workers
City contractors must provide water, shaded rest areas, and additional breaks during an alert.

Section 4: Limitations
Cooling centers may reach capacity, and shuttle service does not cover every neighborhood. Residents should check the live capacity map before traveling.

Section 5: Privacy
Cooling-center staff record only aggregate visitor counts. They must not collect immigration status or ask for proof of residency.
