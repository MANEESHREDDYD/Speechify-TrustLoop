Title: Designing for Dyslexic Learners

Section 1: Reduce decoding pressure
Audio narration can reduce the effort spent decoding words so learners can focus on concepts and meaning.

Section 2: Keep visual context
Synchronized highlighting helps learners keep their place and connect spoken language with written text.

Section 3: Check comprehension
Short source-grounded questions, recaps, and learner explanations reveal whether listening produced real understanding.

Section 4: Respect privacy
Personalization should use the minimum learning data required and give learners control over stored history.
