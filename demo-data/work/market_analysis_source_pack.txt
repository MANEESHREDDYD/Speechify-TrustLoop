Title: Voice-first Productivity Market Analysis

Section 1: Market Overview
Voice-first productivity tools are expanding from transcription and dictation into AI agents that summarize, research, write, and prepare work deliverables.

Section 2: User Personas
Students need faster study workflows. Professionals need meeting notes, document summaries, and reports. Enterprise teams need auditability, privacy, and collaboration.

Section 3: Synthetic Competitors
ReadFlow AI focuses on document reading and summaries.
DictaNote Pro focuses on voice typing and grammar cleanup.
PodBrief turns documents into short audio briefings.
MeetingMind focuses on meeting notes and action items.
VoiceDesk focuses on customer support voice agents.

Section 4: Feature Gaps
Many tools generate content but do not show whether the output is grounded in source material. Few tools expose claim-level evidence or missing-topic detection.

Section 5: Enterprise Risks
Enterprise users need data privacy, access controls, audit trails, citation quality, and reliable evaluation of AI outputs.

Section 6: Accessibility Opportunity
Voice-first tools can help users with dyslexia, ADHD, low vision, and reading fatigue. The product should support personalization without exposing sensitive data.
